# NHI-Sentinel — Non-Human Identity Governance Platform 

Automated discovery, risk scoring, and AI-powered policy remediation across Entra ID, Okta, and Active Directory.

## Project Overview

Most IAM programmes focus entirely on human users, but **Non-Human Identities (NHI)** — service accounts, API bots, OAuth apps, and automation credentials — are typically the largest and least-governed identity population in any enterprise. They don't log in via MFA, they don't get offboarded when a project ends, and they rarely appear in access reviews. A single overprivileged service account or a leaked CI/CD token can be the entry point for a lateral movement or data exfiltration attack.  

**NHI-Sentinel** is a locally-hosted identity governance platform that discovers, inventories, and continuously monitors non-human identities across Entra ID, Okta, and Active Directory. It surfaces orphaned accounts, overprivileged tokens, and exposed secrets, assigns a real-time risk score to every identity, and uses a locally-running LLM (Llama 3 via Ollama) to generate least-privilege policy recommendations — which can be exported as JSON, Terraform, or PowerShell and deployed directly to the connected identity provider. No identity data leaves the machine.

## Feature Walkthrough

### Executive Overview

The landing dashboard provides an at-a-glance posture summary for the entire NHI estate — total identities, active risk alerts, secrets in scope, and policies enforced — giving an executive or security lead an immediate signal on programme health without needing to drill into individual connectors.

![NHI-Sentinel Executive Overview dashboard](<./screenshots/Screenshot 2026-05-27 114044.png>)

---

### Identity Inventory

The Identity Inventory module enumerates every non-human identity discovered across all connected sources. Each record shows identity type (service account, API bot, OAuth app, certificate), originating connector, last activity, owner mapping, and whether the identity has an active business justification. Orphaned and shadow identities — those with no owner or no last-login record — are surfaced here for review.

![Identity Inventory showing all discovered NHI records across connectors](<./screenshots/Screenshot 2026-05-27 114122.png>)

---

### Behavior & Risk

The Behavior & Risk module analyses each identity's activity patterns against its granted permissions. Identities that have not been active within a configurable window, that are using a small fraction of their granted scope, or whose behaviour has deviated from baseline are flagged for remediation. Each identity receives a numeric risk score that feeds the platform's overall posture rating.

![Behavior & Risk module showing anomaly detection and risk scoring per identity](<./screenshots/Screenshot 2026-05-27 114134.png>)

---

### Exposure & Secrets

The Exposure & Secrets module scans connected repositories, configuration files, and environment stores for hardcoded credentials, leaked API keys, and exposed tokens. Findings are prioritised by severity and mapped back to the owning identity so that each secret exposure is actionable rather than just a raw finding list.

![Exposure & Secrets module showing secrets detected with severity and owner mapping](<./screenshots/Screenshot 2026-05-27 114145.png>)

---

### Policy Workbench — Review Queue

The Policy Workbench surfaces identities flagged as overprivileged and presents the current permission state alongside the AI-recommended least-privilege policy. Each finding is tagged High or Reviewing, and the analyst can inspect the AI's reasoning before deciding to accept or modify the recommendation.

![Policy Workbench review queue showing flagged identities with High and Reviewing status](<./screenshots/Screenshot 2026-05-27 114204.png>)

---

### Policy Workbench — Code Export & Deployment

Once a recommendation is accepted, the workbench exports the remediated policy as JSON, Terraform, or PowerShell. The AI note embedded in the export documents the reasoning: *Downgraded Owner → Reader. Removed staging scope. Added dormancy condition. Llama 3 confidence: 97%.* Policies are pushed directly to the identity provider via the Deploy button, and the Deployment Log tracks every push — `AD\backup-runner` and `ci-github-actions` both show **Success**.

![Policy Workbench showing AI-generated JSON export and successful deployment log](<./screenshots/Screenshot 2026-05-27 114224.png>)

---

### Security Reports — Posture Summary

The Reports module produces a period-over-period security posture summary exportable as PDF. For the Apr 7 – May 6, 2026 period: overall risk score **47 / 100** (Medium Risk, −31% vs prior period), 100 identities scanned (+3 new), 7 critical alerts (4 resolved), 14 secrets exposed (+2 new), 23 policies enforced (+5 added). The Risk Score Over Time chart shows the trajectory from 72 → 47, with a stated target of below 30 by end of Q3 2026.

![Security Reports Posture Summary showing risk score 47 and trend chart from 72 to 47](<./screenshots/Screenshot 2026-05-27 114242.png>)

The second half of the report breaks down risk by source connector and identity type. ## Key Concepts Demonstrated

| Concept | How It Is Demonstrated |
|---|---|
| **NHI Governance** | Full identity lifecycle applied to machine identities — discovery, classification, risk review, remediation, and audit |
| **Shadow Identity Detection** | Orphaned and unowned credentials surfaced via cross-referencing active credentials against owner and last-activity records |
| **AI-Powered Least Privilege** | Llama 3 (local, via Ollama) generates scoped-down role assignments with documented reasoning and confidence score |
| **Multi-Platform Enumeration** | Single platform aggregates identities from Entra ID, Okta, and Active Directory into a unified inventory |
| **Privilege Right-Sizing** | Permissions granted vs. permissions used compared per identity; over-privilege flagged with specific remediation action |
| **Secret & Credential Exposure** | Automated detection of hardcoded secrets and leaked tokens mapped back to owning identity |
| **Risk Scoring** | Numeric risk score per identity and aggregate posture score with period-over-period trending |
| **Policy-as-Code** | Remediated policies exported as JSON, Terraform, or PowerShell and deployed directly to identity provider |
| **Local AI Privacy** | All LLM inference runs on-device via Ollama; no identity data transmitted to external APIs |
| **Audit & Reporting** | PDF-exportable Security Reports with posture summary, risk breakdown, and top-risks remediation queue |
